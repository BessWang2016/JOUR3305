Journalism Practicum (Data)

JOUR3305

Aims & Objectives

Students learn to report, edit, and produce the experimental data news website Data Story in either English or Chinese and manage its associated social media platforms.

Course Content

Under supervision, each student is required to work as a reporter and an editor for Data Story. Students are expected to submit story ideas and form groups to work on selected stories under the guidance of the course adviser. They will produce data-driven journalistic stories for the website and its associated social media platforms. Periodic feedback sessions will be held by the adviser.

The course is structured around a project-based workflow. Each group will treat story-making as a project, going through the Preparation, Analysis, Conduct, and Evaluation (PACE) process. Each member will take on varied roles including:

  • Data Collecting & Cleaning
  • Data Wrangling & Analysis
  • Data Visualization
  • Drafting Stories & Reporting
  • Interface Design & Publishing

Students are expected to regularly update the website by uploading edited stories and are also expected to produce multimedia versions of their data stories.

Learning Outcomes (CILOs)

By the end of the course, students should be able to:

  • CILO 1 Conduct data-driven reporting assignments independently and competently by producing output suitable for a variety of digital and traditional news media.
  • CILO 2 Establish relationships with news sources.
  • CILO 3 Edit and produce multimedia pieces to professional standards for a variety of digital and traditional news media.
  • CILO 4 Learn to finish work under a strict deadline.
  • CILO 5 Show the importance of teamwork in news production, especially a data journalism project involving varied professional skills.

Assessment Methods

Student Portfolio

50%

A portfolio of work, including at least two data stories, graded on quality of reporting, editing, and production.

Story Ideas / Pitch

20%

One individual story pitch evaluated for news value, originality, and creativity.

Participation & Teamwork

30%

Evaluated on effort, contribution in meetings, and effective team communication. Peer reviews may be considered.
